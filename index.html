Creating A Legend

Matthew Murdock was blinded as a result of exposure to radioactive materials at the age of nine. His father, Battlin' Jack Murdock, was a boxer that refused to back down. Orphaned as a result of the rampant crime in Hell's Kitchen, one would expect him to give up... not today, he said.
Daredevil, with his heightened senses, fighting skills and thirst for justice, has become one of the most influential heroes of all time. Created by Stan Lee and Bill Everett and improved upon by the legendary Frank Miller, he is as much a symbol of hope and security as all the Avengers combined.

Pillar of Law

As the Daredevil, he has saved countless lives and brought a sense of safety to nightime Hell's Kitchen. However, the ever unwearied spirit of Matt Murdock displays a proficiency not just on the battlefield, but also in a court of Law.
A skilled orator and well versed in the Law, Matt Murdock has undertaken the defense of New York's innocent in the daytime as well. He has protected the civil rights and liberties of common New Yorkers on numerous occasions and has brought the criminals of the day to justice.
From defending tenants against greedy landlords to teaching his clients how to defend themselves once he couldn't, the list of what Matthew has done for his city is far from short.
